PRIVACY POLICY
Data Governance Framework (UK GDPR)

01. Introduction

Vectixore LTD ("we", "our", or "us") respects your privacy. This policy explains how we handle your personal data when you visit our website or use our software applications.

We operate in strict compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

02. Data We Collect

Depending on how you interact with our services (e.g., website inquiries or app usage), we may collect:

  • Identity Data: Name, username, or unique identifiers.
  • Contact Data: Email address (for support inquiries).
  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type, device model, and operating system versions necessary for software compatibility.
  • Usage Data: Analytics on how you use our digital products.

03. Legal Basis for Processing

We only process your data when we have a lawful basis:

  • Contractual Necessity: To provide the services you requested.
  • Legitimate Interests: To improve our software, prevent fraud, and ensure network security.
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with UK tax and corporate laws.

04. Data Storage

Vectixore LTD is a UK-based entity. Your data is stored on secure servers. We do not sell your personal data to third parties. Any international transfers are protected by appropriate safeguards such as UK International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTA).

05. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to access, correct, erase ("right to be forgotten"), and restrict the processing of your data. To exercise these rights, please contact us.

06. Contact Us

Controller: Vectixore LTD

Email: info@vectixore.com

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you believe your rights have been violated.

07. Notice to EU Residents (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the following provisions apply in addition to our standard policy:

  • Legal Rights: You have the right to exercise your data protection rights under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation EU 2016/679).
  • Supervisory Authority: While our lead authority is the UK ICO, you maintain the right to lodge a complaint with your local Data Protection Authority (DPA) in your country of residence.
  • Cross-Border Transfer: Transfers of your data from the EEA to the UK are currently covered by the EU Commission's "Adequacy Decision" regarding the UK's data protection standards.
